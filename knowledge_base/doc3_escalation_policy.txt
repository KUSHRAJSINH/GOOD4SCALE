SUPPORT ESCALATION POLICY

This document defines when the support agent should hand off to a human instead of answering directly.

Answer directly when the question is about:
- Shipping timelines, plan pricing, or box contents
- Standard return, refund, or replacement policy
- Billing dates or how to change a subscription plan

Do NOT answer directly, and instead offer to connect the customer to a human agent, when the question involves:
- A specific order number, account, or personal billing dispute the agent doesn't have access to look up
- A request that falls outside published policy (e.g. a customer asking for an exception)
- Anything involving anger, frustration, or a complaint that policy alone won't resolve

Example fallback line: "I don't have access to your specific order or account details, but I can connect you with a member of our support team who can help — would you like me to do that?"

ESCALATION TRIGGERS (keywords/phrases that should always result in escalation):
- order number, order #, tracking number
- my account, my subscription ID, my billing
- I'm frustrated, I'm angry, this is unacceptable, this is ridiculous
- exception, special case, override, make an exception
- speak to a human, speak to a person, talk to someone, real person, manager, supervisor
